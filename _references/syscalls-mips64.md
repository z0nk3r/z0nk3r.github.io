*AKA: `MIPS64`, `MIPS III`, `MIPS64r2`.*

*CPU ISAs: `mips64`, `mips64el`.*

## Calling Convention

| Syscall NR | Return | arg0 | arg1 | arg2 | arg3 | arg4 | arg5 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `v0` | `v0` | `a0` | `a1` | `a2` | `a3` | `a4` | `a5` |
{: style="--table-col-1: 12.5%; --table-col-2: 12.5%; --table-col-3: 12.5%; --table-col-4: 12.5%; --table-col-5: 12.5%; --table-col-6: 12.5%; --table-col-7: 12.5%; --table-col-8: 12.5%;"}

Invoked via `syscall`. Unlike the 32-bit o32 ABI, n64 fits all 6 args in registers — no stack spill. `a3` doubles as a boolean error flag: nonzero means `v0` holds a positive errno instead of the return value.

## Syscalls

*Note: argument types are sourced from each syscall's man page, not copied from a cheat sheet. Syscall numbers are the n64 ABI's absolute values (kernel table offset + 5000), matching what real MIPS64 n64 assembly places in `$v0` — a separate table from the 32-bit `mips` (o32) page, not just a wider version of it. `mips64` and `mips64el` share this exact table — endianness doesn't change syscall numbers or registers, only byte order.
